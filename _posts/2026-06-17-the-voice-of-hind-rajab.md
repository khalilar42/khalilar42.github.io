I just finished watching the movie "The Voice of Hind Rajab".

A 6 years old child was on call with the Palestinian Red Crescent Society for 3 hours asking to be rescued.

She was in a car surrounded by 6 dead family members corpses.

The Palestinian Red Crescent Society fought for hours for permission from the Israeli military to make an 8-minute drive to save Hind Rajab.

Once they got the green light they sent the ambulance following the specific route **given by the Israeli military force**.

At 200m of reaching Hind, an explosion was heard in the ambulance dispatcher.

Hind Rajab dead body was found roughly about 12 days later, along side 6 of her other family members, along side the paramedics inside the ambulance at approximately 50m.

[**335 bullets**](/assets/img/2026-06-17-the-voice-of-hind-rajab/335-bullet-illustration.png){: w="700" h="900" } were shot on the car where Hind Rajab was in.

**Since Israel's military campaign in Gaza began after the October 7, 2023 Hamas attack, approximately 20,000–21,500 children were killed, we know Hind Rajab's name because her final moments were heard by the world. But she was only one child.**
[**Whenever I hear this audio of Hind Rajab**](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zknke1P2tus), **my mind cannot grasp the enormity of the stories of all the others whose names we will never hear and I keep asking myself: What if I had been there? What if Hind Rajab had been my child? If the pain of imagining the loss of one child is almost unbearable, if the death of one child can haunt me this deeply, I cannot begin to comprehend the grief carried by the families of thousands.**
